Source: Quora - "What are some tips and hacks for incoming freshmen at University at Buffalo?"
URL: https://www.quora.com/What-are-some-tips-and-hacks-for-incoming-freshmen-at-University-at-Buffalo
Type: Student advice forum

Tip 1: Live on North Campus if you're a freshman. The majority of freshman classes take place on North Campus, and having to take the bus every day and night between campuses is a nightmare. Save yourself the commute and live where your classes are.

Tip 2: Avoid 1 Capen (Student Services) during the first week of classes. It will be absolutely packed and you'll be waiting in line for hours. If you have any administrative issues, try to resolve them before the semester starts or wait until the second week when the rush dies down.

Tip 3: Meet with your academic advisor often to ensure all of your program requirements are being met. Don't assume you're on track just because you're passing classes. Advisors can catch issues early, like missing prerequisites or general education requirements you didn't know about.

Tip 4: If you're struggling in classes, there are tons of free resources available: tutors, office hours, study groups, and academic support programs. Don't wait until you're failing to get help. UB has tutoring centers for most major subjects, and professors' office hours are underused by most students.

Tip 5: Submit your housing application as early as possible. Priority for selecting your on-campus housing location is based on the date you submit your Housing Application, so it's best to submit right away to get the dorm or building you want.

Tip 6: Get involved early. With over 500 clubs and organizations on campus, joining something in your first semester helps you make friends fast. Join something related to your major for networking, and something related to a hobby for balance and stress relief.

Tip 7: Take advantage of the UB Stampede shuttle service to get between North and South campus. The buses run regularly, but plan extra time during peak hours because they can get crowded, especially in the first few weeks.

Tip 8: Don't buy textbooks until after the first week of classes. Many professors will tell you which books are actually required vs. recommended. You can save hundreds of dollars by waiting, buying used copies, or finding free PDFs online.
