Graduate Student Life Hacks at University at Buffalo:

Hack 1: Use the Graduate Student Association (GSA) for funding. The GSA offers travel grants, professional development funds, and event sponsorship that many grad students don't know about. Apply early each semester because funds run out.

Hack 2: The campus libraries are open late and have quiet study spaces that are way better than trying to work from your apartment. Lockwood Library on North Campus and the Health Sciences Library both have extended hours during finals.

Hack 3: Get to know the department administrative staff. They know everything about deadlines, forms, funding opportunities, and departmental politics. Being on good terms with them can save you massive headaches.

Hack 4: The campus gym (Alumni Arena) is free for all students. It has a pool, weight room, basketball courts, and fitness classes. Working out regularly is one of the best things you can do for your mental health during grad school.

Hack 5: If you're an international grad student, the mall and market shuttle is a lifesaver for groceries. It runs weekly and takes you to nearby stores, which saves you from needing a car.

Hack 6: Cook in bulk on weekends. Grad student stipends don't go far, and eating out in Buffalo adds up fast. The ethnic grocery stores on Niagara Falls Blvd have affordable ingredients for international cuisines.

Hack 7: Take advantage of the free counseling services. Grad school is stressful, and there's no shame in talking to someone. Counseling Services at UB offers free sessions for registered students, including group therapy and wellness coaching.

Hack 8: Network outside your department. Attend interdisciplinary events, join clubs, and go to campus talks. Some of the best professional connections come from people outside your immediate research area.

Hack 9: Buffalo winters are brutal. Invest in a good winter coat, waterproof boots, and learn to layer before November hits. The wind chill on North Campus is especially bad because it's so open and exposed.
