Living and Dining at University at Buffalo:

Housing Options:
Students can choose between on-campus and off-campus living. On-campus housing offers convenience, safety, and community. UB has residence halls on both North and South campus, as well as apartment-style housing for upperclassmen.

North Campus residence halls include Ellicott Complex and Governors Complex. Ellicott is the larger of the two and houses most freshmen. Governors is smaller and quieter, preferred by students who want a calmer environment. Both complexes have their own dining halls, convenience stores, and study lounges.

South Campus has older but character-rich dorms closer to the city and the South Campus dining options. Students living on South Campus should be prepared for a 20-minute bus ride to most classes on North Campus.

Dining Program:
UB's dining program is ranked #1 among SUNY University Centers. The culinary team crafts menus centered around the freshest, highest-quality ingredients available, with innovative cuisine accommodating various tastes and dietary needs, including healthy balanced options.

Dining Locations:
- Crossroads Culinary Center: The main dining hall on North Campus, located in Ellicott Complex. Features multiple stations with international cuisine, comfort food, vegetarian and vegan options, and a salad bar.
- One World Cafe: Offers cultural cuisine from around the world.
- C3 (formerly known as Putnam's): Located in Governors Complex.
- Big Blue and Little Blue food trucks: Offer creative dining experiences on campus at various locations.

Meal Plans and Payment:
Meal plans and campus cash options are available through the UB Card. The Mobile GET app enables contactless transactions at dining facilities. Access dining options and meal plan information at myubcard.com.

UB Card Office: 716-645-2521, ubcard-info@buffalo.edu

Shopping on Campus:
North Campus has UB Commons with retail shops and restaurants. The University Bookstore carries textbooks, school merchandise, magazines, and has a post office. Convenience stores in Governors and Ellicott Complex residential halls carry snacks, drinks, and basic necessities.

Hand-decorated cookies featuring the UB logo are a beloved campus tradition available at the dining centers.
