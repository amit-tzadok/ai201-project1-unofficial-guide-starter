LeetCode and Technical Interview Preparation for UB CS Students:

When to Start:
If you're a first-year student, you probably haven't had a chance to learn advanced algorithms yet and that's totally okay. Start with the topics you've already learned — arrays, strings, basic loops — and work on those problems by tag on LeetCode. After CSE 250 (Data Structures), you'll be able to tackle medium-difficulty problems. After CSE 331 (Algorithms), you'll have the foundation for most interview questions.

Recommended Timeline:
- Freshman year: Learn basic programming well (CSE 115/116). Start exploring LeetCode Easy problems for fun.
- Sophomore year: After CSE 250, begin serious LeetCode practice. Focus on arrays, linked lists, trees, hash maps, and basic sorting. Aim for 2-3 problems per week.
- Junior year: After CSE 331, you'll understand dynamic programming, graph algorithms, and complexity analysis. Ramp up to 4-5 problems per week. Target medium-difficulty problems.
- Senior year: Maintain your skills. Focus on hard problems and system design if targeting senior-level positions.

Key Topics to Study:
1. Arrays and Strings — most common interview topic
2. Hash Maps and Sets — appear in almost every interview
3. Trees and Binary Search Trees — know traversals, BFS, DFS
4. Linked Lists — pointer manipulation, fast/slow pointer technique
5. Dynamic Programming — hardest topic, but learnable with practice (start after CSE 331)
6. Graphs — BFS, DFS, shortest path algorithms (covered in CSE 331)
7. Sorting and Searching — binary search is especially important
8. Stack and Queue problems
9. Recursion and Backtracking
10. System Design — for more senior roles, understand how to design scalable systems

Study Resources:
- LeetCode (free tier is sufficient to start, premium helps with company-specific questions)
- NeetCode.io — curated problem lists organized by topic, with video explanations
- Blind 75 — a list of 75 essential LeetCode problems that cover all major patterns
- Cracking the Coding Interview (book) — good for understanding interview structure
- UB ACM sometimes hosts mock interview sessions and coding workshops

Behavioral Interview Tips:
Technical interviews aren't just about coding. You'll also face behavioral questions. Prepare stories about teamwork, handling conflict, and times you overcame a technical challenge. Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

Common Mistakes:
- Waiting until junior year to start preparing. Start early, even if it's just a few problems a week.
- Only doing Easy problems. You need to be comfortable with Medium to pass most interviews.
- Not practicing out loud. In real interviews, you need to explain your thinking while coding.
- Ignoring time complexity. Always analyze the Big O of your solution.
