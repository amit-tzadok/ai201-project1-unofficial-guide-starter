Student Parking and Transportation at University at Buffalo:

General Rules:
All students are allowed to have a car on campus, including first-year students in residence halls. Students must register their vehicle's license plate through the E-Business Center to obtain a virtual parking permit. The parking permit is included as part of the transportation fee, so there's no additional cost. Faculty permits are not valid in student lots, and student permits are not valid in faculty lots.

First-Year On-Campus Residents:
First-year students living in residence halls have specific parking restrictions:
- Must park in their residence hall lot or Park and Ride locations Monday through Friday, 7:00 AM to 3:00 PM.
- Governors Complex residents must use Governors E Lot.
- Should use campus buses and shuttles for getting to class rather than driving.
- Permitted Park and Ride lots on North Campus: Center for Tomorrow, Arena, Alumni B & C, Stadium.
- Permitted Park and Ride lots on South Campus: Main-Bailey, Parker.

Non-First-Year Residents:
Non-first-year on-campus residents may park in any student-designated lot Monday through Friday, 7:00 AM to 3:00 PM, except commuter-only lots (Jarvis A, Hochstetter B on North Campus). University apartment residents need virtual permits specific to their complex.

Commuter Students:
Off-campus students may use any student-designated lot, including commuter-specific lots like Jarvis A and Hochstetter B.

Buses and Shuttles:
Bus and shuttle stops are conveniently located at or near all of UB's official on-campus residence halls and apartment complexes. Real-time tracking is available via smartphone or computer. The UB Stampede shuttle runs between North and South campus regularly. The mall and market shuttle provides weekly shopping service for groceries and mall trips.

Bicycling:
UB provides biking resources including route information, winter storage facilities, and cycling support through bike registration and bikeshare programs.

Transportation Resources:
Buffalo also offers public buses, Metro Rail, and the NFTA (Niagara Frontier Transportation Authority) system. Students can use their UB Card for discounted transit.
