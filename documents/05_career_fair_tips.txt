UB STEAM Job + Internship Fair and Career Preparation:

About the Fair:
The STEAM Job + Internship Fair is UB's largest career event, connecting 120+ employers with 3,000+ UB students interested in Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics fields. It's held annually and is the single best opportunity to meet recruiters face-to-face. Even if you're not actively job hunting, attending gives you practice talking to companies and understanding what they're looking for.

Preparation Checklist:
1. Register through Bullseye (powered by Handshake) to access fair details and employer lists.
2. Research employers before the fair — know what each company does before approaching their booth. Don't ask "What does your company do?" — it shows you didn't prepare.
3. Bookmark your target employers and check their qualifications and job/internship requirements.
4. Use the fair map to plan which booths to visit. Popular companies get long lines, so prioritize.
5. Upload your resume to Big Resume for AI-powered feedback.
6. Make your Bullseye profile public so employers can find you after the fair.
7. Practice your elevator pitch — a 30-second introduction covering your name, major, year, and what you're looking for.
8. Dress professionally. Business casual at minimum, business formal is better.
9. Bring 15-20 copies of your resume printed on good paper.
10. Update your LinkedIn profile and connect with recruiters after the fair.
11. Download the Handshake app for streamlined booth check-ins and follow-ups.

Interview Preparation Resources:
- Big Interview: Online video interviewing tool where you can create practice sessions and rehearse at home.
- Career Design Center offers one-on-one practice interview appointments — to maximize readiness, use both tools.
- The Career Design Center also helps with resumes, cover letters, LinkedIn profiles, networking strategies, and job search skills.

Tips from Recruiters:
- Give a confident elevator pitch that highlights the most remarkable thing you've done — a project, research, or internship experience that shows you're passionate about technology.
- Make eye contact, be confident, and don't be afraid to approach employer booths and ask questions.
- Follow up with a thank-you email within 24 hours of meeting a recruiter. Mention something specific from your conversation.
- If you're a freshman or sophomore without much experience, emphasize your coursework, personal projects, and eagerness to learn.
