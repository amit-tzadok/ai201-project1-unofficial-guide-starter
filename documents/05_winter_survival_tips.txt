Five Tips for Managing Buffalo Winters (Written by a UB Student):

Tip 1: Dress in Layers
Invest in sweaters and a snow jacket. Temperatures can drop to 5 degrees Fahrenheit or lower. Wear thermal wear beneath sweaters for maximum warmth. Layering is the key to survival in Buffalo! You'll need colorful sweaters for approximately 8 months of cold weather — yes, winter lasts a long time here.

Tip 2: Get Winter Accessories
Waterproof, insulated boots with good traction are essential because roads become very slippery. Quality snow gloves and hats protect against frostbite and windburn. The goal is to expose as little skin as possible when walking between buildings on campus. The wind chill on North Campus is especially harsh because the campus is flat and open with few windbreaks.

Tip 3: Prioritize Skin Care
Moisturize daily to prevent flaking and dryness from the cold, dry air. Use lip balm regularly to avoid chapped lips. This applies equally to everyone — the dry heated indoor air and cold outdoor air will destroy your skin if you don't take care of it.

Tip 4: Consume Warm Foods and Beverages
Drink hot beverages and eat calorie-dense foods since cold weather makes your body temperature drop and you'll feel an urge to eat more. Stay hydrated despite reduced winter water intake — room heaters and heavy clothing layers actually cause dehydration.

Tip 5: Stay Active
Exercise combats seasonal depression (SAD), boosts energy, and prevents winter weight gain. Getting outdoors for workouts provides additional mood benefits, even in winter. The campus gym at Alumni Arena is free for all students and has great facilities.

Bonus Activities: Enjoy winter in Buffalo with ice skating at Canalside, skiing and snow tubing trips (the Schussmeisters Ski Club offers affordable group trips), sledding at Chestnut Ridge Park, visiting Niagara Falls in winter (it partially freezes and looks incredible), and exploring local coffee shops to warm up.
