My First Buffalo Winter at University at Buffalo:

Support for International Students:
The UB Office of International Student and Scholar Services (ISSS) helps non-U.S. students acclimate to campus life and Buffalo winters. They offer immigration assistance, Social Security and driver's license application help, safety information, guidance on U.S. academic culture, and tax filing support. The office also helps students find housing and locate necessary winter gear and familiar comfort foods. If you're an international student, ISSS should be one of your first stops.

Cold-Weather Activities Organized by ISSS:
- Skiing and snow tubing trips to local mountains
- Professional hockey games (the Buffalo Sabres play downtown)
- Workshops on driving in snow and winter safety
- Social gatherings to help international students build community during the isolating winter months

University-Wide Winter Celebrations:
- Polar Plunge (December): Team UB participates in Western New York's Special Olympics fundraiser with a swim in Lake Erie. It's freezing, it's wild, and it's a beloved UB tradition.
- Winterfest (February): A 60+ year tradition featuring broomball, ice bowling, ice skating, igloo-building contests, sleigh rides, food, and music. This is one of the biggest social events of the winter semester.

The Schussmeisters Ski Club:
This student-run club offers affordable ski lessons, social events, and group trips to local ski mountains like Holiday Valley and Kissing Bridge. It's one of the best ways to actually enjoy winter instead of just enduring it.

Real Student Experience:
A Colombian Fulbright graduate student shared this story: "One day my wife and I woke up and saw that the car was 100% frozen, and we were late for classes. The main problem was that we didn't have the special tool to remove the ice from the windshield, so I had to use a spatula from the kitchen because it was the hardest sharp thing I found." Lesson: Buy an ice scraper before your first winter. Keep it in your car along with a small shovel, jumper cables, and an emergency blanket.

Local Winter Activities:
Cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, attending hockey games at KeyBank Center, sledding at Chestnut Ridge Park, and visiting Niagara Falls in winter are all popular activities among UB students. Buffalo actually embraces winter — there's always something to do if you're willing to bundle up.
