Course Registration and Academic Advising at University at Buffalo:

Step 1: Resolve Any Holds
Before registration opens, check HUB Student Center for any holds on your account. Holds prevent course enrollment, grade access, and even diploma release. Common holds include outstanding financial obligations or failing to submit required documents like immunization records. Clear these well before your enrollment appointment.

Step 2: Know Your Enrollment Appointment
Every student receives a specific date and time when they can begin registering for classes. View this in the HUB Student Center under the Manage Classes tile. Mark your calendar and register as soon as your window opens — popular classes fill up fast, sometimes within hours.

Step 3: Schedule Academic Advisement
Meet with your academic advisor through the Navigate app before your enrollment date. Your advisor will help you explore your major, understand degree requirements, and review your plan for next semester. Students should meet with their advisor at least once a semester until graduation. Don't skip this — students who skip advising are more likely to take unnecessary courses or miss prerequisites.

Course Planning Tools:
- Schedule Builder: Search for courses and plan your weekly schedule. You can import courses directly from your academic planner.
- Shopping Cart: Place desired courses and validate them to check for time conflicts or prerequisite issues before registration opens.
- Path Finder: Select UB Curriculum Pathway courses and save them to HUB.
- Class Schedules: View registration numbers, class times, locations, instructor names, and seat availability.
- Academic Advisement Report (AAR): Track your progress toward degree completion. Check this every semester.

Credit Hour Guidelines:
- Full-time status: 12 or more credits per semester.
- Recommended pace: 15 credits per semester to graduate in 4 years.
- Maximum: 19 credits during fall and spring semesters. More than 19 requires advisor approval.

Pro Tips:
- Allow 1 hour travel time between North and South campuses when scheduling classes. Allow 30 minutes between classes within North Campus itself — it's a big campus.
- Changes are permitted through the drop/add period at the start of each semester.
- Use Rate My Professors or ask upperclassmen about professors before choosing sections. The same course can be a completely different experience depending on who teaches it.
- If a class you need is full, check back during the first week — students drop classes constantly during add/drop.
