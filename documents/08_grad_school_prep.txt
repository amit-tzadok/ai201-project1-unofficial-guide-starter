Graduate School Preparation for UB CS Students:

Should You Go to Grad School:
Graduate school in CS is not for everyone. An MS is useful if you want to specialize in an area like AI/ML, security, or systems — or if you want to advance into more senior technical roles. A PhD is for people who want to do research, work in academia, or pursue research positions at top labs. If you're happy with a software engineering career, a bachelor's degree plus strong internship experience is often sufficient.

UB's MS in Computer Science and Engineering:
The program is STEM-approved and currently has 384 master's candidates. It offers 11 specializations:
1. AI and Machine Learning
2. Computer Security and Privacy
3. Computer Vision and Image Processing
4. Database Systems
5. Distributed Systems and Networks
6. Embodied AI (Robotics)
7. High Performance Computing
8. Information Retrieval / NLP
9. Programming Language Design
10. Pure and Applied Logic
11. Software and Hardware Systems

Program Tracks:
The Research Track is more selective and involves in-depth learning, culminating in either a capstone project (presented at CSE Demo Day) or a thesis defense. This track is ideal for current UB undergrads who want to continue directly into the MS program.

BS/MS Combined Program:
UB offers a streamlined BS/MS program that takes 134 credits total instead of 150 credits if done separately. You get guaranteed graduate admission if you maintain satisfactory progress. This is one of the best-kept secrets for UB CS undergrads — you can finish both degrees in 5 years instead of 6.

Application Requirements:
- Two letters of recommendation for MS, three for PhD
- GRE is NOT required for either program
- Letters from professors who know your academic work are strongly preferred — this is where research experience and TA relationships pay off

How to Prepare as an Undergrad:
1. Do undergraduate research (CSE 498/499). This is the single most important thing for grad school applications.
2. Build relationships with professors — you need strong recommendation letters.
3. Maintain a good GPA, especially in core CS courses. Aim for 3.5+ if possible.
4. Take challenging electives in your area of interest to show depth.
5. Read research papers in your area of interest. Start with survey papers to understand the landscape.
6. Attend research talks and seminars in the department — they're free and expose you to cutting-edge work.
7. If applying to PhD programs elsewhere, start the process in fall of your senior year. Deadlines are typically December.

Career Outcomes:
MS graduates from UB CSE have been hired at major companies including Bloomberg, Google, and Microsoft. Computing and IT occupations had a median income of $82,860 nationally. Faculty in the department bring in an average of $4.5 million annually in external research awards, which funds graduate assistantships.

Funding:
Graduate assistantships (TA positions) provide a stipend and tuition waiver. Research assistantships (RA positions) are funded through faculty grants. External fellowships like NSF GRFP are highly competitive but cover full tuition plus stipend for 3 years.
