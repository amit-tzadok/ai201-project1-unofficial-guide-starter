Mental Health and Wellness Resources at University at Buffalo:

Counseling Services Overview:
Counseling Services promotes the personal well-being and academic success of students by providing brief mental health services, educational programs, crisis intervention, and campus community consultation. Whether you're worried about grades or going through a personal crisis, these services are available to all currently registered students at no additional cost.

Services Available:
- Individual counseling sessions with licensed psychologists, mental health counselors, and social workers
- Group therapy sessions on topics like anxiety, depression, and stress management
- Wellness coaching to help set and achieve meaningful personal goals
- Psychiatric nurse practitioners for medication management if needed
- Graduate student interns also provide counseling under supervision

Embedded Counselors:
UB has placed counselors directly within all 12 academic units and the athletics department. This means you can see a counselor who understands the specific pressures of your program — an engineering student faces different stressors than a nursing student. This is a relatively unique program that many students don't know about.

Mental Health Topics Covered:
- Stress Management: Counseling for anxiety and stress affecting academics and relationships
- Depression: Professional support when negative thoughts impact your wellbeing and daily functioning
- Body Image and Eating Disorders: A dedicated Eating Disorders Team handles anorexia, bulimia, and binge eating
- Grief and Loss: Support for processing death and grief
- Substance Use: Resources for students dealing with alcohol or drug concerns

Crisis Resources (available 24/7):
- Counseling Services: 716-645-2720 (after hours: press option 2 for the crisis counselor)
- 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline: Call or text 988
- Crisis Text Line: Text 741741
- Students of Concern reporting system for when you're worried about a fellow student
- Victim Assistance for unwanted sexual experiences
- University Police: 716-645-2222 (or use blue light emergency phones on campus)
- Off-campus emergencies: 911

Self-Help Resources:
UB provides online tools and awareness resources to support emotional health between counseling sessions. The "What the Health" e-newsletter offers ongoing wellness tips and event listings.

Wellness Events:
The counseling center hosts regular wellness events including stress-relief activities during finals week, mental health awareness workshops, and mindfulness sessions. Check the wellness events calendar on the Student Life website.
