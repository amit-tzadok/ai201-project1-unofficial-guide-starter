Off-Campus Housing Guide for University at Buffalo Students:

Before You Start Looking:
Start your housing search early. Apartments near campus fill up quickly, and rental rates can increase as the start of the school year draws closer. The best deals on apartments near UB are usually found in March and April for the following fall. Waiting until summer means slimmer pickings and higher prices.

Safety First:
Only rent properties that have passed a safety inspection by a New York State certified inspector within the last three years, or where the owner lives on-site. New York State law requires a smoke detector in each bedroom and a carbon monoxide detector on every floor with sleeping areas. Check the batteries at the start of each semester. If your apartment has any health or safety violations — inadequate heating, dangerous wiring, mold, or pests — these can legally justify lease termination.

Before Signing a Lease:
Read the lease carefully and understand every clause. Visit and inspect the apartment in person. Evaluate location, neighborhood safety, proximity to bus routes and UB shuttle stops, parking availability, and overall condition. Find out about the landlord before signing — ask current tenants about their experience. Connect with student club members or upperclassmen for references on specific buildings or landlords.

Key Questions to Ask Your Landlord:
- How quickly do they respond to emergency repairs?
- What is the security deposit amount and what is the typical refund rate?
- Will locks be changed before you move in?
- Are utilities included? What are average monthly costs for heat, electric, water, and internet?
- What is the condition of appliances and laundry facilities?
- What are the garbage collection and recycling procedures?

Roommate Agreements:
Sign a roommate contract with all roommates, even if you are friends. This lets you set clear expectations about rent splitting, cleaning responsibilities, guests, noise levels, and shared expenses. Be aware that each tenant on a lease bears responsibility for the entire rent amount, not just their individual portion — if your roommate doesn't pay, you could be on the hook.

Paying Rent:
Use a check or money order to pay rent — never cash. If there is ever a dispute, it's difficult to prove you paid someone in cash. Standard move-in costs include first month's rent, last month's rent, and a security deposit.

When You Move In:
Take photos of every room and document any existing damage. Complete an apartment inventory and condition report. This protects your security deposit when you move out. Follow up any verbal complaints to your landlord with email documentation for a paper trail.

Renters Insurance:
Your landlord's insurance does not cover your personal belongings. Renters insurance protects against theft, fire, and damage, and also provides liability coverage. It's inexpensive (often $15-25 per month) and highly recommended.

Popular Areas Near UB:
Students commonly live in Amherst (closest to North Campus), the University Heights neighborhood near South Campus, Tonawanda, and Kenmore. Each has different tradeoffs in terms of rent, walkability, nightlife access, and proximity to campus shuttles.

Transportation from Off-Campus:
Buffalo offers public buses through NFTA, Metro Rail, UB shuttles between campuses, bike registration, and bikeshare programs. Having a car is helpful but not essential if you live near bus routes.

Important Reminders:
- Register your current local address each semester through HUB Student Center
- The Code of Student Conduct applies to all students regardless of where they live
- Follow local trash disposal rules, noise ordinances, and alcohol laws
- Be a good neighbor: introduce yourself, keep noise down, and maintain your property

For housing complaints, contact Erie County Department of Health, the 311 Resolution Center, or the New York State Attorney General's office.
