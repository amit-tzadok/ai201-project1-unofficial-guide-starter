Clubs, Activities, and Getting Involved at University at Buffalo:

Overview:
UB offers over 500 clubs and organizations across campus. Getting involved outside the classroom is one of the best things you can do as a student — it helps you make friends, pursue your passions, gain leadership experience, and build your resume.

How to Find and Join Clubs:
UBLinked is the primary hub for student involvement. You log in with your UB IT credentials and can browse all registered clubs, discover events and programs, track volunteer hours, access your co-curricular transcript, and check in to events via QR code. Every official club at UB is listed on UBLinked.

What to Join:
The university recommends getting involved in four types of organizations:
1. Something related to your major or career goals — for networking and professional development
2. Something related to a hobby or passion — for balance, stress relief, and meeting people with shared interests
3. Something outside your comfort zone — to grow as a person and try new things
4. Something focused on health and wellness — to stay physically and mentally healthy

Student Government:
UB has seven affiliated student governments that recognize and fund clubs:
- Undergraduate Student Association (SA) — the main undergraduate government, funds most undergraduate clubs
- Graduate Student Association (GSA) — funds graduate student organizations and events
- Medical Student Polity (MSP)
- Graduate Management Association (GMA) — for School of Management students
- Student Bar Association (SBA) — for law students
- American Student Dental Association (ASDA)
- School of Pharmacy Student Association (SPSA)

Starting a New Club:
If no existing club matches your interests, you can start your own. Requirements:
- 10 founding members
- Approval from a recognizing agent (student government, university department, or campus ministry)
- Registration through UBLinked with constitutional documentation
- Recognition typically takes 7-14 days after submission

Popular Types of Clubs:
- Cultural and identity organizations (dozens of cultural groups representing UB's diverse student body)
- Academic and pre-professional clubs (pre-med, engineering, business, etc.)
- Sports and recreation clubs (intramural sports, club sports, outdoor adventure)
- Arts and performance groups (theater, a cappella, dance, music)
- Community service and volunteering organizations
- Gaming and esports clubs
- Political and advocacy groups
- Religious and spiritual organizations
