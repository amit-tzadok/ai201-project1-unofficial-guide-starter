The UB Campus Life Experience:

Campus Culture:
UB is a large research university with over 30,000 students, which means there's always something happening but it can also feel overwhelming at first. The campus is split between North Campus (main, suburban, most classes) and South Campus (closer to the city, more historic). Most freshmen live and take classes on North Campus.

Things to Do on Campus:
- Alumni Arena: Free gym, pool, basketball courts, fitness classes, and rock climbing wall
- Student Union: Recently renovated, has restaurants, a game room, meeting spaces, and student organization offices
- Center for the Arts: Hosts concerts, plays, dance performances, and art exhibitions — many events are free or discounted for students
- Harriman Hall game room: Pool tables, ping pong, and video games
- Abbott Library special collections: Hidden gem for book lovers, has rare manuscripts and historical documents
- Esports lounge: Competitive gaming facilities
- Campus bikes: Available for getting around campus quickly
- Drone shows and DJ performances at campus events

Hidden Gems:
- The Relaxation Lounge: An on-campus oasis with quiet retreats for decompressing between classes
- Food trucks (Big Blue and Little Blue): Show up at different locations around campus — follow UB Dining on social media to find them
- The Marching Band (Thunder of the East): Even if you don't play an instrument, going to games and hearing them play is a huge part of the UB spirit experience

Getting Around Campus:
UB Stampede shuttle runs between North and South campus on a regular schedule. Real-time tracking available on your phone. During peak hours (especially 9 AM - 12 PM on weekdays), expect crowded buses. Walk if the weather allows — North Campus takes about 15-20 minutes to cross on foot.

Support Resources:
- Student Life Office: 520 Capen Hall, (716) 645-6125 — your go-to for any student life questions
- Career Design Center: Resume building, job search help, internship connections, mock interviews
- Accessibility Resources: Accommodations for students with disabilities
- Intercultural and Diversity Center: Programs and community for underrepresented students
- LGBTQ+ Center: Safe space and resources
- Orientation and Transition programs: Help with adjusting to college life
- Parent and Family Programs: Resources for families of students
- University Police: 716-645-2222 (or blue light emergency phones across campus)
- Off-campus emergencies: 911

Safety:
North Campus generally feels very safe, with well-lit pathways and blue light emergency phones. South Campus and surrounding city areas require more awareness, especially at night. Use the UB shuttle and don't walk alone after dark in unfamiliar areas. The university offers a safe walk program and campus escorts.

The Student Guide (buffalo.edu/studentlife) serves as a one-stop online source for information related to life outside the classroom, covering everything from new student orientation to career preparation through the "Here to Career" employment readiness program.
